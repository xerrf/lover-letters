Dear Github

Like father like son, isn't that right Github? I should have known that his relationship wasn't going to work. You make everything so public for the whole world to see. I've got a repository of memories of you and none of them are good. Just like your son, you could never commit to me. I tried so hard to push you in the right direction and when I threatened to leave, you acted as if you changed. But I won't be fooled anymore, every time you pull me back I realize that nothing I did mattered, like the changes I pushed for had been overwritten.

Late at night, I would see my phone light up with the message, “README” and I would fall for it, but never again, I'm not interested in a relationship of collaboration between more than two people.

You look like a stalker when you follow all these people on your social feature, watching their repos grow into big projects. You even get notififications about their activity and can see people that they follow. I guess that's how you found me so I shouldn't be surprised that you are still lurking about.

Well, it's done. I am done with your family forever!
